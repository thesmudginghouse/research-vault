The first Sissen known to our family was Julian Frederick Sissen. He was born about 1810 in France. These facts seems to be untrue. I cannot find any Julian documentated.

The name Sissen was spelt many different ways eg. Sisson, Scisson, Syson, Sisen, Sessions etc. Mom used to write to Granny Eliza Jane Sissen in England when she was still alive and she always reminded her that Sissen was spelt with an "e" not an "o". According to Aunt Maud, there was a falling out with the "o" Sissons. So they changed their name.

I have been searching through censuses in England and cannot find any information on Julian Frederick Sissen Senior. Yet in Granny Eliza Jane's birthday diary there is a mention of Julian Frederick Sissen died June 19,1886. Julian Sissen was born January 20,1895 in Manningtree Essex County England. Julian joined the army and reached the rank of Lance Corporal in the 9th Squadron of the MachineGun Corps (Calvary). His service number was 50736. H was killed in action at the age of 23 on March 22, 1918. I had found a real account story of a soldier from the 9th Squadron whom died in battle the same day.

PRIVATE HERBERT GEORGE COLUMBINE 9th Squadron (Cavalry)

Born 28.11.1893 / Died 22.3.1918 Date of Gazette 3.5.1918

On 22nd March 1918 at Hervilly Wood, France, Private Columbine took over command of a gun and kept firing it from 9 am to 1 pm in an isolated position with no wire in front. During this time wave after wave of the enemy failed to get up to him, but at last with the help of a low flying aircraft the enemy managed to gain a strong foot holding in the trench.

Maude Mary Sissen born May 27, 1899

Maude Mary Sissen was born May 27, 1899 in Gas Works, Oxford Road, Manningtree, England. Oxford Road can be found on the Manningtree Map and situated on the bottom of the side of the page. Aunt Maude, as I knew her was the only Sissen that I had known. With exception of my grandmother, her older sister, Edith. Maude was a very special person, who was warm, bubbly, full of life and always laughing. After her husband George had passed away. We started to see more of her at our house. At least that is when I started to know her better. She always told stories but whenever I asked her about our family tree she would she you don't want to know. That there were skeletons in the closet, that there were gypsies in our family. Yet, Aunt Maud used the term gypsies as being bad people. As the story went that the first Sissen, left France and went to England was because they had murdered someone.

In fact the Gypsies used to come into town and Aunt Maude used to always visit them. And when she was young, her mother Eliza Jane would take he to see them so she could play with the other children. These children were actual cousins of the Eliza's side of the family.

Julian's side of the family had wealth and wanted nothing to do with her side of the family. Aunt Maude use to visit the grandfather Sisson (They spelt the name Sisson with the O and Aunt Maude was spelt with and E) Eliza always reminded my Mother of that during correspondence with her while Ernie was in England during the war.

Maude married George Hanning at the St.Clement Church, Ashton Lane
