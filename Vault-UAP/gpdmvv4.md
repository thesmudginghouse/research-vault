- Recurrent atmospheric plasma phenomena (APP) — earth lights, earthquake lights, persistent luminous orbs — share a common physical substrate: peroxy defect (p-hole) charge carrier chemistry present in essentially all crustal igneous and high-grade metamorphic rock (Freund mechanism).

- A complete causal chain links solar weather to ground-level plasma discharge: CME → Forbush decrease (48-96h) → ionospheric TEC anomaly (24-72h) → Kp threshold → atmospheric E-field coupling via water table → Pathway 6 streaming potential modulation → APP discharge.
- APP color is a direct spectral readout of local mineral/atmospheric chemistry at the discharge point — decades of "unreliable" eyewitness color reports become an inadvertent geological survey dataset.
- v2.4 formally adds trees (bioelectric generators via sap-flow streaming potentials and piezoelectric wood) and mycorrhizal fungal networks (documented memristor/capacitor/sensor properties, 100Hz–10kHz signal propagation) as biological Variable 3/4 modifiers, forming a complete biological-geological circuit from crustal discharge zone to canopy corona point.

## METHODOLOGY

GPDM is a literature-synthesis and predictive-classification framework, not a new empirical dataset. It integrates the Freund p-hole mechanism (2002-2019) with piezoelectric, triboelectric, electrochemical, radon/dusty-plasma, and seismoelectric literatures into a unified four-variable state equation Φ(output) = f(V1,V2,V3,V4), where V1=stress rate, V2=carrier density/lithology, V3=atmospheric state (bifurcation variable), V4=geometric confinement/circuit closure. Six geological pathways are classified by dominant mechanism. Validation is proposed via a standardized 5-9 sensor monitoring package (ULF magnetometer, air ion counter, IR thermal camera, calibrated color camera, EFM, optional Geiger/radon/geophone/GPS) deployed at three anchor sites (Brown Mountain NC, Hessdalen Norway, La Peña de Juaica Colombia) plus secondary candidate sites (Dyatlov Pass/Mt. Kholat Syakhl, Taro Valley Italy, Southern California fault zones, Uinta Basin/Skinwalker Ranch).

## DEFINITIONS

- **P-hole / Peroxy defect (PHP):** Positive hole charge carrier formed when hydroxyl pairs in silicate minerals undergo redox conversion during cooling (600-400°C), creating O3Si-OO-SiO3 peroxy links — latent charge capacity in nearly all crustal igneous/metamorphic rock.
- **Battery mode vs. plasma burst mode:** Battery mode = sustained sub-threshold h• outflow producing continuous low-amplitude signals (hours-months); plasma burst mode = h• concentration exceeds critical threshold in confined asperity, bursts explosively (co-seismic EQL).
- **Schottky barrier:** Boundary between stressed/unstressed rock that passes h• carriers but blocks electrons — produces unipolar magnetic pulse signature.
- **Seismoelectric effect:** Acoustic stress wave through water-saturated porous rock induces relative motion between rock matrix and ionic pore fluid, generating streaming potentials (electric dipoles) detectable at surface.
- **APP:** Atmospheric Plasma Phenomena — the umbrella term for earth lights, earthquake lights, and persistent luminous orbs covered by GPDM.
- **OR logic structure:** The state equation operates such that any single pathway reaching activation threshold is sufficient for observable output — pathways are not required to co-occur.
